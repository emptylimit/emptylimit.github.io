Functors Preserve Isomorphisms

Written by: Gabriel Field.

Theorem statement

Theorem.

Let $F : \mathsf{C} \to \mathsf{D}$ be a functor and let $i : x \simeq y$ be an isomorphism in $\mathsf{C}$. Then, $F\ i : F\ x \simeq F\ y$ is an isomorphism in $\mathsf{D}$.

Direct proof

One has $F\ i \circ F(i^{-1}) = F(i \circ i^{-1}) = F(1_y) = 1_{F\ y}$ and similarly $F(i^{-1}) \circ F\ i = 1_{F\ x}$; hence, $F\ i$ is an isomorphism with inverse $F(i)^{-1} = F(i^{-1})$.
